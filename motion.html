Motions

A motion is a formal way that a delegate can instigate an action. There are many kinds of motions that have many different purposes. When a delegate would like to put a motion forward, he/she/they would simply say "I motion to ….”.


  • Formalities


    1. Motion to begin session-Motion to begin session is a parliamentary procedure used to officially start a meeting or session.

    2. Motion to begin roll call - Any delegate may motion for a roll call vote; this will be adopted automatically – the delegate can either say present or present in voting. Remember you cannot shift back to present after saying present in voting, but you can change from present to present in voting any time. It is preferred to remain as present in voting throughout the session if you are a first timer.

    3. Motion to adjourn session -you can rise your placard and say “motion to adjourn session”and if it passes by chair’s discussion and gets majority voting in the committee your motion passes.

    4. Motion to suspend committee – if you want to suspend the committee temporarily for a very important reason like discussing important amendments and if it passes by the chair’s discussion and it gets majority voting in the committee then the motion is passed. This motion is generally not passed if the committee is short on time.

    5. Motion to close committee - it is used if you want to end formal committee session.
