What is Caucus?

In Model United Nations (MUN), a caucus refers to a moderated or unmoderated session during which delegates engage in discussions, negotiations, and collaborations on specific topics or issues. Caucuses allow delegates to work together in smaller groups, either informally or formally, to address specific aspects of a resolution, propose amendments, or negotiate solutions to problems.a


What are the types of Caucus?

Caucus is of two types:
  1. Moderated Caucus(mod cauc)-formal session
  2. Unmoderated Caucus(unmod)-informal session



What is Moderated?

A moderated caucus is a formal debate on particular subjects that might be of interest to the committee as a whole:
  • Speaking time is the allocated amount of time for each delegate to address the committee on a given topic, cannot exceed the limits set by the executive board.
  • It is usually 30-90 seconds per speaker. Total time: is the overall amount of time provided for discussion of the specified topic.
  • Raising a motion for a mod cauc:
    1. Step 1 – rise your placard, and wait to be addressed by the EB.
    2. Step 2 – After being addressed rise a motion saying – MOTION TO ENTER MODERATED CAUCUS
    3. Step 3 – if passed by the chair’s discussion, then name the topic of the moderated caucus and give the no. of speakers and the time allotted to each speaker
    4. Ex : 16/1 which means there will be 16 delegates speaking and the time for each speaker is 1 minute
    5. The committee will vote on each motion and it will need a simple majority to pass. A moderated caucus shall end when there are no more delegates who wish to speak on the subject at hand, regardless of the remaining time
    • Way to move committee into a certain direction


What is Un-moderated?

The objective of an unmoderated caucus is to stimulate discussion through the use of an informal debate.
  • Delegates are not needed to speak in a formal manner or with the Chairperson’s approval when participating in an Unmoderated Caucus.
  • The entire committee discusses a topic togethere
  • No decorum (but you are marked)
  • For a fixed time period. No topic anything can be discussed and everyone can speak together
  • Keep an underhand on negotiation
  • If the EB believes there is no longer any worthwhile discussion, they may also quickly adjourn an unmoderated caucus.
  • How to enter an unmoderated caucus (if the floor for motions is open)
  • Step 1 – rise your placard, and wait to be addressed by the EB.
  • Step 2 - After being addressed rise a motion saying – MOTION TO ENTER UNMODERATED CAUCUS and specify the time (no need to specify topic as the committee can discuss any topic of their choice) Ex: 30 minutes
  • Step 3 – if passed by the chair’s discussion, the committee will officially enter into informal session or unmoderated caucus.
